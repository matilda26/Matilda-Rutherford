Banana-Haus

Art

Collaborative project with artist, Geoff Nees, Banana-haus looks at the contribution the post second world war wave of European migration had on Melbourneâ€™s design culture. Fleeing the destruction of postwar Europe, many artisans and craftspeople found themselves disembarking in an antipodean cultural blank canvas. Their contribution still resonates to this day.

The banana-haus chair takes an iconic and anonymous piece of vernacular outdoor furniture from the dross of the Australian leisure-scape and reimagines it in a more noble framework.

Echo

Product

Using critical design, echo is a comment on a possible shift to a narcissistic society as a result of increasing interaction with social media. Despite the negativity towards narcissism, it is emerging as a social alternative and, thus, should be embraced as a new commercial market.

Using facial recognition software, the fluid mirror analyses facial features and transforms them into a unique soundscape. Thus luring the user into their own image.

The I'mbrella - an oversized umbrella for one - is a comment on how our narcissistic tendencies are impacting the way in which we interact with others in public social situations.

Mary Lou

Product

Inspired by the modern cowboys of the Western world, Mary Lou was an exhibition piece for Dowel Jones, a furniture design Studio at RMIT run by Dale Hardiman and Adam Lynch of Lab De Stu.

With the raw dowel and laced leather thonging holding the natural leather saddle in place, Mary Lou takes you on a leisurely ride through the modern cowboy.
